Pollution

How it's slowly killing our home

Overview

Pollution is the introduction of any harmful material into the environment. It can be anything from chemicals, to plastic, or even gases.There is about 5.25 trillion pieces of trash in the ocean, with a combined weight of approximately 269,000 tons. Not only does the pollution the ocean directly harm the marine life, the food and rescources we take from the ocean are also affected by plastic.

The picture on the right is an image of the Great Pacific Garbage Patch, a massive collection of trash in the Pacific Ocean. It is estimated to be about 1.6 million square kilometers, and is made up of about 80,000 metric tons of plastic.

The most obvious cause of pollution is the massive amount of trash that gets thrown in the water every year. But as mentiened earlier, there's more than one kind of pollution.   • Sewage and Wastewater
